How to Set Up a Meeting in Microsoft Teams

Published: January 2026

Use this guide to schedule meetings in Teams. Set details, add participants, and choose options.

What you'll need

  • A Teams account and access to the desktop or web app
  • Email addresses for attendees
  • Accurate date, time, and time zone
  • Reliable internet connection

Schedule a meeting in Teams

  1. Open Teams and go to Calendar.
  2. Select New meeting.
  3. Enter a meeting Title.
  4. Set the date, Start time, and End time.
  5. Add Attendees by entering their email addresses.
  6. Add details in Description (agenda or notes).
  7. Select Send to deliver the invite.

Schedule a meeting in a channel

  1. Go to Calendar and select New meeting.
  2. Enter the meeting Title.
  3. Select Add channel (or choose a Channel).
  4. Pick the Team and Channel for the meeting.
  5. Set date and time; add Attendees if needed.
  6. Select Send. A post appears in the channel.

Meeting options

  • Lobby: choose who can bypass the lobby.
  • Presenter: control who can present or share content.
  • Recording: allow recording or start automatically (if available).
  • Recurrence: set the meeting to repeat (daily, weekly, custom).

Verify your meeting is scheduled

  1. Confirm the meeting appears in Calendar.
  2. Open the invite and verify time and time zone.
  3. Check that attendees received the invite and the link opens Teams.
  4. If scheduled in a channel, confirm the post is visible.
  5. Run a test join to check audio and video devices.

Troubleshooting

Invite not delivered

  • Cause: Incorrect email or notifications blocked.
  • Fix: Verify addresses; resend the invite; ask recipients to check spam/junk.

Cannot join the meeting

  • Cause: Outdated app or sign-in issue.
  • Fix: Update the app; sign in; try the web app; switch networks if needed.

No audio or video

  • Cause: Permissions denied or wrong device selected.
  • Fix: Allow mic/camera; choose the correct devices in settings; run a test call; reconnect your headset.
